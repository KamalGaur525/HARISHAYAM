Care & Maintenance of Your Marble Ganesha Statue

Proper care and maintenance are essential to preserve the beauty and longevity of your marble Ganesha statue from Harishyam Arts. By following these guidelines, you can ensure that your statue remains in pristine condition for years to come.

  •  Dusting: Regular dusting helps keep your marble Ganesha statue clean. Use a soft, dry cloth or a microfiber duster to gently remove dust from the surface. Avoid using abrasive materials or rough cloths that could scratch the marble. Dusting once a week is usually sufficient to maintain the statue's cleanliness.

  • Cleaning: For a deeper clean, use a mild soap solution. Mix a few drops of pH-neutral soap with distilled water. Dampen a soft cloth with this solution and gently wipe the statue's surface. Be careful not to saturate the marble, as excessive moisture can lead to staining. After cleaning, use a clean, dry cloth to remove any soap residue and moisture.

  • Stain Removal: If your marble Ganesha statue gets stained, it's important to address it promptly. Blot any spills immediately with a soft cloth. For stubborn stains, use a poultice made from baking soda and water. Apply the poultice to the stained area, cover it with plastic wrap, and let it sit for 24 hours. Remove the poultice and rinse the area with distilled water, then dry it thoroughly.

  • Polishing: To maintain the statue's shine, consider periodic polishing. Use a marble-safe polish and a soft cloth to gently buff the surface. Avoid over-polishing, as it can damage the marble over time. Polishing once or twice a year is usually sufficient to keep the statue looking its best.

  • Protection: Place your marble Ganesha statue in a location where it is protected from extreme temperatures, direct sunlight, and heavy humidity. These environmental factors can cause discoloration and damage to the marble. Additionally, consider using a base or pedestal to elevate the statue, protecting it from accidental bumps or knocks.

Handling: When moving the statue, handle it with care. Lift it from its base, avoiding pressure on any delicate parts. It's advisable to have assistance when moving larger statues to prevent accidents or damage.

By following these care and maintenance guidelines, your marble Ganesha statue will remain a cherished and beautiful part of your home for many years to come.
